How to Use SnapAssist

To search for photography locations for the subject matter of your choice, type your keywords into the box. SnapAssist will then perform a keyword search to find the relevant photos in its database, cluster the photos using their geolocations, and plot the photos and clusters on a map.

On the map, the centre of each cluster is denoted with a blue pin. Clicking the pin expands it to give summary statistics and representative photos (and their EXIF data). The cluster's longitude and latitude are determined by averaging the long-lat of the cluster's photos, weighted by the number of views each photo has. The cluster's rank (#1, above) is determined by ordering the clusters' average number of views per photo divided by the number of photos in the cluster (a joint measure of the quality of the photos taken at a location and how original the location is).

How SnapAssist Works

For information on how SnapAssist works, and how to deploy it, please see its GitHub page.
